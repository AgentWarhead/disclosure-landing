# DISCLOSURE Intel Article Doctrine

Every `/intel` article must work as a civilian first-contact briefing, not a lore dump.

## Reader promise
Give a curious civilian a clear answer, a useful action, and a reason to enter the Disclosure funnel.

## Voice
- Plain, tense, cinematic, but understandable on the first read.
- Treat extraordinary claims as scenarios, reports, theories, or lore unless grounded in official public record.
- No fake certainty where the topic is speculative.
- Use short paragraphs, concrete examples, and field-guide language.
- Avoid jargon without translation.

## Required structure
1. **Direct answer opening:** Say what the article is about and why it matters in the first 2 paragraphs.
2. **Why people search this:** Address the obvious search intent quickly.
3. **Practical civilian value:** Include what to do, what not to do, or how to think clearly.
4. **First-contact readiness:** Tie the topic to calm, documentation, role awareness, or decision-making.
5. **Disclosure app bridge:** CTA should connect article topic to classification quiz, First Contact Card, or app access.
6. **Related files:** Add internal links to relevant `/intel`, `/first-contact`, `/archetypes`, and `/quiz` pages.

## SEO standard
- One clear H1 matching the primary query.
- Meta title under roughly 60 characters when possible, with `| DISCLOSURE`.
- Meta description around 140-160 characters, written for clicks.
- H2s should match natural questions people search.
- Use the primary keyword in the opening paragraph.
- Include 3-6 related keywords naturally, not stuffed.
- Add FAQ schema when the article answers direct questions.
- Keep OG/Twitter titles aligned with the article promise.

## Conversion standard
Each article should offer standalone value, then invite the reader deeper:
- Awareness articles: link to `/first-contact`, `/quiz`, and one practical guide.
- Species articles: link to `/intel/types-of-alien-species-ranked-threat/`, `/quiz`, and `/archetypes`.
- Protocol articles: link to `/quiz`, `/readiness`, and `/first-contact`.
- Government/history articles: link to practical preparation, not just more history.

## Content rules
- No internal SEO labels or scaffolding visible to users.
- No unreadable faint text.
- No em dashes in public copy.
- No reckless promises about safety, health, law, or guaranteed outcomes.
- Keep the classified aesthetic, but make the instruction human.
